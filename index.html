About / Overview

Our laboratory focuses on next-generation artificial intelligence with an emphasis on robustness, scalability, and practical impact. We connect methodological innovation with real-world applications in multimodal understanding, distributed intelligence, and reliable AI deployment.

We are interested in how AI systems can learn from multiple data sources, collaborate across decentralized environments, and make reliable predictions under uncertainty. Our work spans fundamental machine learning, algorithm design, and deployment-oriented research. We welcome students and collaborators from machine learning, computer vision, signal processing, data mining, and related areas.

Research

We organize our research around several closely connected themes in modern AI.

Multimodal Learning

Learning from text, audio, video, images, and other heterogeneous signals for sentiment analysis, emotion recognition, and intelligent perception.

Federated Learning

Privacy-aware and communication-efficient learning over distributed clients, especially under Non-IID and personalized settings.

Bayesian AI

Bayesian deep learning, posterior inference, and reliable uncertainty estimation for scalable and trustworthy prediction.

Sequential and Temporal Modeling

Time series analysis, temporal point processes, state space models, and dynamic multimodal systems.

Reliable AI Systems

Robustness under modality dropout, temporal misalignment, noisy supervision, and real-world deployment constraints.

Human-Centered AI Applications

AI methods for healthcare, consumer electronics, multimedia understanding, and broader intelligent decision support systems.
